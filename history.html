History of Aberdeen

Founded in 1175

How was Aberdeen founded?

There have been people living in Aberdeen since the stone age, and the city was split into two: Old Aberdeen and New Aberdeen. Old Aberdeen hosted the university and the cathedrals whereas new Aberdeen was where fishing occurred. Aberdeen was first settled in 6000 BC, Romans arrived in Aberdeen and fought and defeated the united armies near the peak of benachie.

The start of civilisation...

Roberts the Bruce transformed Aberdeen into a property owning and independent community, Aberdeen played a big role in Roberts life as they protected him during wars. Old Aberdeen was burned by Edward 111 of England but was soon rebuilt and extended, and for a while after Aberdeen were under attack by neighbouring lords.

The 18th to 21st century...

The hospital was built as well as an asylum. The council improved roads by opening George Street, union street and king street, many new streets and houses were formed, as well as the council. Aberdeen is know filled with families upon different generations and is a home to many people. The castles affected by different attacks still remain as monuments and are available to visit.
